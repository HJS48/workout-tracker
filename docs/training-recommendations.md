## 1. Recommended Weekly Structure: 4 Gym + 2 Run + 1 Cycle

Your current 3+3 split has two problems: Push day is too long (80 min), and you need more pressing/pulling frequency for your primary goals. The recommended restructure:

| Day | Session | Duration | Key Focus |
|-----|---------|----------|-----------|
| Mon | **Push A** (gym) | 40-45 min | Heavy bench press, incline DB, tricep work |
| Tue | **Hard run** | 35-45 min | Intervals (odd weeks) / tempo (even weeks) |
| Wed | **Pull A** (gym) | 40-45 min | Weighted pull-ups, rows, face pulls |
| Thu | **Zone 2 cycling** | 45-60 min | HR 130-135 bpm, steady state |
| Fri | **Push B + Pull B** (gym) | 45-50 min | Close-grip bench, BW pull-ups AMRAP, accessories |

**Why this is better:**
- Sessions drop to 40-50 min (from 80 min)
- Bench frequency increases to 2-3x/week (from 2x)
- Pull-up frequency increases to 2-3x/week with mixed loading (weighted + bodyweight)
- Cycling replaces the easy Thursday run, protecting your shins while building the same aerobic base
- Running drops from 3 to 2 sessions — appropriate for your current injury status and training history
- Interference effect is minimized: cycling produces less interference than running (Wilson 2012 meta-analysis)

**Optional add:** Grease the groove pull-ups on non-gym days — 3-5 sets of 6-8 reps spread through the day if you have access to a bar.

## 2. What's Missing From Your Current Program

### Critical gaps (add immediately)

**A. Rotator cuff & scapular work — you have ZERO**
For an athlete chasing 150kg bench, this is the single biggest gap.
- Face pulls: 3x15-20, every gym day (as filler between sets)
- Band pull-aparts: 2x20, warm-up
- External rotation (side-lying DB or cable): 2x15, twice per week

**B. Close-grip bench press**
Tricep lockout strength is the most common limiter above bodyweight bench. Pushdowns and skull crushers aren't specific enough.
- Add: Close-grip bench or Spoto press, 3x6-8, once per week (replace incline BB on Push+Pull day)

**C. Weighted pull-ups**
Training only bodyweight to failure will plateau. Increasing absolute strength makes bodyweight feel lighter.
- Add: Weighted pull-ups, 4x5-6 with +10-15kg on Pull day. Keep BW AMRAP on the other day.

**D. Calf & tibialis work (shin splint prevention)**
At 92kg, your calves absorb 225-275kg per footstrike (2.5-3x bodyweight). They need dedicated training.
- Standing calf raises: 3x10-12
- Seated calf raises: 3x12-15 (targets soleus — the primary running muscle)
- Tibialis raises: 2x15-20 (direct MTSS prevention)
- Goal benchmark: 30+ single-leg calf raises

**E. Single-leg work**
Leg press + leg curl with no squats/deadlifts leaves gaps in hip stability, single-leg strength, and running economy.
- Add: Bulgarian split squats or reverse lunges, 3x8-10 per leg
- Add: Barbell hip thrusts, 3x8-12 (your only hip extension movement)

### Lower priority adjustments

- Reduce dragon flags from 3 sessions to 2 (6 sets/week core is generous for your goals)
- Reduce lateral raises from 3 sessions to 2 — replace the 3rd with face pulls
- Straight arm pulldown is lowest priority back exercise — can be dropped entirely

## 3. Bench Press Programming Path

### Phase 1 — Linear progression (now to ~110-115kg e1RM)
Current approach (+2.5kg when all sets hit 6 reps) is sound. Will likely stall within 2-4 mesocycles at roughly 1.2-1.25x bodyweight.

### Phase 2 — Daily Undulating Periodization (115 to ~130-135kg)
When linear stalls, switch to DUP (Zourdos 2016 showed it superior to linear periodization for trained males):
- Day 1: Heavy — 4-5x3-5 @ 80-87% 1RM
- Day 2: Volume — 3-4x8-10 @ 65-72% 1RM
- Day 3: Moderate — 3-4x6-8 @ 72-80% 1RM

### Phase 3 — Block periodization (130kg+)
Accumulation/transmutation/realization blocks per Issurin (2010).

### Bench press technique cues
- Grip width: ~1.5x biacromial width
- Elbows tucked 45-60 degrees
- Touch lower on chest (nipple line or below)
- Retracted + depressed scapulae throughout
- BB for heavy work, DB for volume/accessories

### Accessory benchmarks that predict a 150kg bench
- Close-grip bench: 130-135kg 1RM
- Incline DB press: 50-55kg DBs x 8-10 (you're at 20kg — long way to go)
- Barbell row: 120-130kg x 5-6
- Weighted dips: BW+40-50kg x 6-8

## 5. Running & Aerobic Development

### Zone 2 volume: how much?
The polarized model (Seiler 2010) shows elite athletes train ~80% easy / 20% hard. For your 5K goal, the research says:
- Minimum 4 aerobic sessions/week for meaningful improvement (Billat 2001)
- Your current 2 easy runs is on the low end — cycling fills the gap
- With the recommended 2 run + 1 cycle structure, you get 3 aerobic sessions (2 Zone 2 + intervals/tempo)
- As shin splints resolve, add a 3rd run to reach 4 aerobic sessions

### AeT development
Your AeT at 73% HRmax has huge room to grow. The primary driver is accumulated time at or below AeT — volume, not intensity.
- Sessions of 60+ min at Zone 2 produce greater mitochondrial biogenesis than shorter sessions (Egan & Zierath 2013)
- Expect AeT to reach ~80% HRmax (153 bpm) within 4-8 months of consistent training (3-4 Zone 2 sessions/week)
- Practically: Zone 2 pace improves from ~7:40/km to ~5:30-6:00/km at the same HR as AeT rises

### Zone 2 pace at 92kg: is 7:40 normal?
Yes. At an AeT of 140 and 92kg, this is expected. A 70kg runner at the same AeT would be ~40-70s/km faster (~6:30-7:00/km). Running economy scales directly with mass — you spend ~31% more energy per km than a 70kg runner. Weight is the biggest variable here.

**Today's run specifically:** Your stride collapsed to 0.95m (from 1.13-1.17m). This was driven by shin pain + deliberately capping HR + fatigue from 4 runs in a week. On a healthy day with no shin issues, your true capped Zone 2 pace would likely be 6:00-6:30/km based on previous isolated laps at HR 130-138.

### Heat adjustment (Brescia, 20-28C)
Train by HR, not pace. Expected pace penalties vs 15C baseline:

Your January 5K (26:19 at 17C) is the valid performance benchmark. Summer runs will always be slower at the same HR — the aerobic adaptations are identical.

## 6. Shin Splint Management

### Why they flared
You went from 1 run per 1-3 weeks to 4 runs in one week. That's a 300-400% volume spike. At 92kg, ground reaction forces are 225-275kg per footstrike. This is textbook MTSS (medial tibial stress syndrome).

### Immediate plan

| Week | Running | Cycling | Total aerobic |
|------|---------|---------|---------------|
| This week | 2 runs, 20-25 min easy | 1x 45 min | 3 sessions |
| Week 2 | 2 runs, 25-30 min | 1x 45 min | 3 sessions |
| Week 3-4 | 3 runs (if pain-free) | 1x 45 min | 4 sessions |
| Week 5-6 | 3 runs | 1x 45 min (or 4th run) | 4 sessions |
| Never | Increase running volume more than 10-15%/week | | |

### When to train through vs stop
- Continue if pain < 4/10, doesn't worsen during the run, subsides within 24 hours
- Stop if pain increases during the run, hurts walking, or persists > 48 hours
- Red flag: localized point tenderness (rule out stress fracture)

### Strengthening protocol (do 3x/week)
**Calf complex:**
- Seated calf raises: 3x15, progress to 3x8 heavy (soleus)
- Standing calf raises: 3x15, progress to single-leg 3x8 (gastrocnemius)
- Benchmark: 25+ single-leg calf raises before increasing run volume

**Tibialis anterior (directly addresses medial shin loading):**
- Tibialis raises (heel on step, raise toes): 3x15
- Toe walks: 3x30 seconds

**Hip stability (poor hip control increases tibial rotation):**
- Single-leg RDLs: 3x8
- Side-lying hip abduction: 2x15

### Cadence intervention
Increase from 137 to 160+ spm. This is the single most evidence-backed gait modification for reducing shin splint loading (Heiderscheit 2011). Shorter, faster steps = less impact per footstrike.

### Cycling as a long-term shin management tool
Yes — cycling at Zone 2 HR (130-135) provides:
- All central cardiovascular adaptations (cardiac output, stroke volume, blood volume)
- ~70% of peripheral adaptations (capillary density, mitochondria — shared muscle groups)
- Zero tibial impact loading
- Less interference with strength gains than running (Wilson 2012)

1 hour cycling ~ 30-40 min running for aerobic stimulus. For Zone 2 HR-matched sessions, the cardiovascular benefit is roughly equivalent.

## 7. The Interference Effect: What You Need to Know

### The good news
Your two primary strength goals (bench, pull-ups) are upper body. The Wilson (2012) meta-analysis and Hickson (1980) both found that running primarily interferes with **lower body** strength and hypertrophy. Upper body is largely protected.

### The real risk
Not molecular interference — it's **systemic fatigue.** Running 3x/week drains recovery that could go to lifting. This manifests as:
- Fewer reps at a given weight
- Worse session quality (your Push day rated 3/5 pump on bench)
- Slower recovery between sessions

### How to minimize it
1. Separate hard sessions by 6+ hours minimum (Robineau 2016). Your alternating-day structure already does this.
2. Never run hard before a key strength session.
3. Eat enough: 2.0+ g/kg protein (184g+/day), 4-5 g/kg carbs (368-460g/day). Carbs are non-negotiable — glycogen depletion amplifies the AMPK interference signal.
4. Sleep 8+ hours. At 6 sessions/week, this is not optional.
5. Deload every 3 weeks initially (3:1 loading:deload).

## 8. Bodyweight: The Central Tension

This is the elephant in the room. Your three goals pull in opposite directions on the scale:

| Goal | Ideal weight | Why |
|------|-------------|-----|
| 150kg bench | 90-95kg+ | Heavier = more absolute strength potential |
| 20 pull-ups | 80-85kg | Lighter = less load per rep. -5kg ~ +2 reps |
| Sub-20 5K | 75-82kg | Every 1kg lost ~ 10-15s faster 5K |

### Recommendation: Phased approach
1. **Months 1-3 (now):** Maintain ~91-92kg. Eat at maintenance. Focus on building training consistency. Do NOT cut during the ramp-up phase.
2. **Months 4-6:** Once bench exceeds 115kg e1RM, begin slow cut: 300-500 kcal/day deficit, protein at 2.2 g/kg. Target: 88-89kg.
3. **Months 7-12:** Continue slow cut toward 85-87kg if bench holds. This is the optimal landing zone — heavy enough for competitive bench strength, light enough for sub-20 5K and 20 pull-ups.

**Impact of dropping from 92kg to 85kg:**
- Pull-ups: +2-3 reps at no strength loss
- 5K: ~1:30-2:30 faster from weight loss alone
- Bench: Slight disadvantage, but offset by accumulated strength gains
