## 1. Philosophy and Evidence Base

### Training Model: Modified DUP + Polarized Running

**Strength:** Daily Undulating Periodization (DUP) for bench press, rotating between heavy (3-5 reps), moderate (6-8 reps), and volume (8-12 reps) days. Research shows DUP produces 25% greater 1RM bench gains vs linear periodization over 12 weeks (Rhea et al., 2002; Miranda et al., 2011). Modified HPS (Hypertrophy-Power-Strength) configuration outperforms traditional ordering (Zourdos et al., 2016).

**Running:** Polarized 80/20 model. Easy runs at conversational pace (Zone 1-2), hard sessions genuinely hard (Zone 4-5). The polarized group in Stoggl & Sperlich (2011) improved VO2max by ~12% over 9 weeks vs threshold or HIIT-only groups.

**Concurrent Training:** Separating strength and endurance sessions by 6+ hours minimizes acute molecular interference (Wilson et al., 2012; Murach & Bagley, 2016). Running interferes with hypertrophy more than cycling, but the athlete's 5K goal demands running-specific work. We mitigate by never running before lower-body or heavy pressing days.

| Pull-ups | 12 reps | 15-16 reps | 3-4 rep improvement in 6 weeks is realistic with dedicated programming. 17 is a stretch. |

## 5. Bench Press Periodization

### Method: Modified DUP (Hypertrophy-Power-Strength Rotation)

Based on Zourdos et al. (2016), the HPS configuration produces greater 1RM gains than traditional ordering. We rotate between two bench days per week:

- **Monday (Heavy/Strength):** Low rep, high intensity — building toward max strength
- **Friday (Volume/Hypertrophy):** Higher rep, moderate intensity — building muscle and work capacity

### 6-Week Bench Press Table

**Current estimated 1RM: 100kg**

| Week | Monday (Heavy) | Friday (Volume) | Est. 1RM |
|------|---------------|-----------------|-----------|
| 1 | 4x4 @ 85kg (RPE 8) | 3x8 @ 70kg (RPE 7) | 100kg |
| 2 | 4x3 @ 87.5kg (RPE 8) | 3x8 @ 72.5kg (RPE 7-8) | 101-102kg |
| 3 | 5x3 @ 87.5kg (RPE 8) | 4x8 @ 72.5kg (RPE 8) | 103-104kg |
| 4 | 4x3 @ 90kg (RPE 8-9) | 3x8 @ 75kg (RPE 8) | 105-106kg |
| 5 (Deload) | 3x3 @ 80kg (RPE 6) | 2x8 @ 65kg (RPE 6) | -- |
| 6 (Test) | Work up to 1RM | -- | **107-110kg target** |

### Bench Press Rules

1. **Warm-up:** Bar x 10, 40kg x 8, 60kg x 5, 75kg x 3, then working sets
2. **If you hit all reps at RPE <8:** Add 2.5kg next week
3. **If you fail a rep or RPE hits 10:** Repeat same weight next week
4. **Rest between heavy sets:** 3 minutes minimum. Use a timer. Do not rush.
5. **Pause reps:** All competition-style. 1 second pause on chest. This builds strength off the chest where most intermediates fail.
6. **Grip width:** Pinky on ring or slightly wider. Consistent every session.

### Why Not Linear Progression?

At 100kg 1RM, linear progression (adding 2.5kg/session) will stall within 2-3 weeks. DUP provides varied stimuli that delay accommodation. The athlete gets heavy practice (neural) on Monday and volume work (hypertrophy) on Friday, covering both primary drivers of strength.

## 6. Pull-Up Programming

### Problem Analysis

The athlete's pull-up profile (12, 9, 7 = 28 total reps with 42% drop-off) indicates:
- **Good peak strength** (12 reps at 92kg BW is solid)
- **Poor muscular endurance** (losing 42% across 3 sets)
- **Likely grip or scapular fatigue** driving the drop-off

### Strategy: Weighted Pull-ups + Submaximal Volume + Optional GTG

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Monday: BW Submaximal Sets** | 4 sets, leave 2-3 reps in reserve each set. E.g., Week 1: 9, 8, 7, 7 (not to failure) |
| **Friday: Weighted Pull-ups** | 4x5-6 with added weight. Builds absolute strength so BW feels lighter. |
| **GTG (Optional, Non-Gym Days)** | 3-5 sets of 5-6 reps spread throughout the day on Tue/Thu/Sat. Never to failure. Never with fatigue. |

### 6-Week Pull-Up Progression Table

| Week | Monday (BW Submaximal) | Friday (Weighted) | GTG (Optional) | Est. Max |
|------|----------------------|-------------------|-----------------|----------|
| 1 | 4x9 (RPE 7) | 4x5 @ +10kg | 4x5 on Tue/Thu | 12 |
| 2 | 4x10 (RPE 7-8) | 4x5 @ +12.5kg | 4x5 on Tue/Thu | 12-13 |
| 3 | 4x10 (RPE 7-8) | 4x5 @ +15kg | 4x6 on Tue/Thu | 13-14 |
| 4 | 4x11 (RPE 8) | 4x4 @ +17.5kg | 5x5 on Tue/Thu | 14-15 |
| 5 (Deload) | 3x8 (RPE 6) | 3x4 @ +10kg | None | -- |
| 6 (Test) | Test max reps | -- | None | **15-16 target** |

### Pull-Up Rules

1. **Full range of motion:** Dead hang at bottom, chin clearly over bar at top
2. **No kipping.** Strict reps only.
3. **Monday sets:** Stop when you feel your form breaking (body swing, chin barely clearing). Leave 2-3 reps in tank.
4. **Weighted sets:** Use a dip belt, not a backpack. Add weight in 2.5kg increments.
5. **GTG sets:** Only on days you feel fresh. Never sore. If you did a hard gym session, skip GTG that day.
6. **Grip:** Alternate between pronated (overhand) and neutral grip if a neutral bar is available. This prevents elbow overuse.

### Addressing the Drop-Off

The submaximal approach (never going to failure on Monday) trains the nervous system to maintain output across sets. By Week 4, the athlete should see significantly less drop-off (e.g., 13, 12, 11 instead of 12, 9, 7) because they've trained consistent set-to-set performance rather than one max set followed by garbage sets.

| Rule | Rationale |
|------|-----------|

## 10. Shin Splint Protocol

### Acute Phase (Weeks 1-2)

The athlete has active shin splints from a 300% volume spike (1 run in weeks -> 4 runs in one week). This is a classic overuse response.

**Daily protocol (every day, including rest days):**

| Exercise | Sets x Reps | Instructions |
|----------|-------------|--------------|
| Tibialis Anterior Raise | 3x20 | Stand with back against wall, feet 30cm forward. Lift toes to shin. Slow and controlled. Can also do off a step. |
| Eccentric Calf Raise | 3x15 | Rise on both feet, lower on one foot over 4 seconds. Each leg. On a step for full ROM. |
| Toe Walks | 2x30 seconds | Walk on heels with toes pointed up. Strengthens anterior compartment. |
| Towel Scrunches | 2x30 seconds | Sit with foot on towel. Scrunch towel toward you with toes. Each foot. |
| Ice massage | 10-15 min | Freeze water in a paper cup. Massage along shin bone after any run or at end of day. |
| Self-massage/foam roll | 5 min | Roll calves and anterior tibialis. Avoid direct pressure on the bone. |

### Maintenance Phase (Weeks 3-6)

- Continue tibialis raises and eccentric calf raises on gym days (built into Lower A and Lower B)
- Ice after any run that produces shin discomfort
- If shins are pain-free for 2 consecutive weeks, can reduce daily protocol to gym days only

### Running Surface Rules

- **Prefer:** Grass, dirt trails, synthetic track, treadmill
- **Avoid:** Concrete sidewalks (highest impact)
- **Asphalt roads:** Acceptable if no better option
- **Shoes:** Ensure running shoes have <500km on them. Consider a more cushioned shoe for easy runs.

### Substitution Rules

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Shin pain at run start | Stop. Bike 30 min at moderate effort (HR 120-140) instead. |
| Shin pain during easy run | Walk 2 min. If pain persists, stop and bike. |
| Shin pain during intervals | Abort session. Easy jog home if tolerable, walk if not. Do NOT push through. |
| Shin pain lasting >48 hours post-run | Drop next week's volume by 20%. Consider seeing a physio. |

## 11. Cadence Improvement Plan

### Current: 137 spm (easy runs) / 149 spm (intervals)

The 137 spm on easy runs is low and means the athlete is overstriding (reaching foot out in front of center of mass), which:
- Increases braking forces (slower)
- Increases tibial stress (shin splints)
- Reduces running economy

### Target Progression

| Week | Easy Run Cadence Target | Method |
|------|------------------------|--------|
| 1-2 | 145 spm | Metronome app (e.g., RunTempo). Set to 145. Focus on quick, light steps. |
| 3-4 | 150 spm | Increase metronome. Add 4x30s high-cadence drills at end of easy runs. |
| 5-6 | 152-155 spm | Continue metronome work. Should feel more natural by now. |

### Do NOT aim for 180 spm.

The 180 spm "ideal" is a myth derived from elite runners at race pace. At easy pace (7:00/km+), 155-165 spm is appropriate for a 188cm tall athlete. Shorter athletes naturally have higher cadence.

### Cadence Drills (2x/week, at end of easy runs)

1. **Quick feet drill:** 4x20 seconds of exaggeratedly quick, short steps. Not faster overall pace — just higher turnover. Walk 30s between.
2. **Downhill strides:** Find a gentle 2-3% downhill. Run 4x60m with gravity assisting higher cadence. Walk back.
3. **Metronome segments:** During easy run, run 2 minutes at metronome target, 2 minutes natural, repeat 4x. Notice the difference.

### Key Cue

"Run like you're on hot coals." Quick ground contact, not reaching forward. Pull foot up, don't push off behind. Land with foot under hips, not in front.

## 13. Deload Strategy

### When: Week 5 (of 6)

### Gym Deload Protocol

| Variable | Normal Weeks | Deload Week |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Volume (sets) | 100% | 50-60% (cut last 1-2 sets per exercise) |
| Intensity (weight) | RPE 7-9 | RPE 5-6 (reduce weight ~10-15%) |
| Exercise selection | Full program | Main lifts only, drop accessories |
| Session length | 45-55 min | 30-35 min |

**Deload week gym schedule:**

- Monday: Bench 3x3 @ 80kg, Pull-ups 3x8 BW, Incline DB 2x8, Rows 2x8. Done in 30 min.
- Wednesday: Leg Press 3x8 @ 100kg, Leg Curl 2x10, Calf Raises 2x15. Done in 25 min.
- Friday: Bench 2x8 @ 65kg, Weighted Pull-ups 3x4 @ +10kg, Dips 2x8. Done in 25 min.
- Saturday: Skip Lower B entirely. Easy run only.

### Running Deload Protocol

- Volume drops ~30% from peak week
- No intervals or tempo
- All runs at easy effort (HR <140)
- Add 4 strides at end of 1 run to keep legs sharp

### Why Week 5 (Not Week 4 or 6)

Week 5 deload allows full supercompensation for Week 6 testing. The body absorbs 4 weeks of progressive overload, recovers in Week 5, and peaks for Week 6 tests. This is standard practice in block periodization.

### Week 6 Schedule

| Day | Test | Protocol |

| Friday | **REST** | Complete rest before 5K |
| Saturday AM | **5K Time Trial** | See pacing strategy below |

### 5K Time Trial Pacing Strategy

**Target: 24:00-24:30 (4:48-4:54/km)**

| Km | Target Pace | HR Zone | Notes |
|----|------------|---------|-------|
| 1 | 4:55/km | Zone 3-4 | DO NOT go out fast. The #1 mistake in 5K racing. This should feel "too easy." |
| 2 | 4:50/km | Zone 4 | Settle in. Find rhythm. |
| 3 | 4:50/km | Zone 4 | Halfway. If you feel good, hold. If dying, you went out too fast. |
| 4 | 4:45/km | Zone 4-5 | Start pushing. This is where fitness matters. |
| 5 | 4:40/km or faster | Zone 5 | Everything left in the tank. Sprint last 200m. |

**Pre-race protocol:**
- Eat a light carb meal 2-3 hours before (toast + banana + coffee)
- 10 min easy jog warm-up
- 4x100m strides with walk-back recovery
- 5 min easy jog
- Start

**Conditions note:** If race day is >25C, add 10-15s/km to targets. Heat is a significant performance limiter and does not reflect true fitness. Consider an early morning (7-8 AM) time trial.
